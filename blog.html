Lessons Learned from a Marketing and Sales Internship

I recently completed a 10-week summer internship with a large tool manufacturing company. It was an incredible learning experience that taught me a lot about sales, marketing, and building relationships with customers. In this blog post, I'll share some of the key strategies I learned that companies can use to drive sales and build their brand.

Developing Product Knowledge

One of the first things I learned was the importance of thoroughly understanding the products you are selling. As the internship progressed, I was trained on all the features and benefits of the various tools the company offered under its different brand names. Things like battery voltage, motor type, special features, target users, and ideal use cases. The deeper my product knowledge, the easier it was to connect customers with the right tools for their needs and price range. Taking time to learn about products allows salespeople to confidently answer questions and guide customers to the ideal solution.

Tracking Market Share Data

Using sales data from the stores, we were able to analyze the market share between our brand and competitors. This enabled us to develop scenarios for how to grow our brands by looking at sales volume and value for different product categories. Access to data provides a compass for improvement.

Building Relationships

Some of the best sales happen when you build relationships with customers and show that you truly want to solve their problems. Taking time to ask questions and listen to needs builds trust and connection. It's also vital to build relationships with personnel inside the stores you work with. Having store managers and employees on your side makes it easier to get things done and improve visibility for your brand.

Leveraging Promotions and POP Displays

Well-designed promotions and point-of-purchase (POP) displays are powerful ways to boost sales. During major promotional events, we would set up large POP displays at the front of the store stocked with discounted tools and accessories. These eye-catching displays would draw customer attention and make it easy for them to find the deals. Promotions create a sense of urgency and excitement that drives sales. Pairing discounted tools with free accessories also encourages customers to spend more. Strategic use of promotions and POP displays can lead to big rewards.
